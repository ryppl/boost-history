Introduction

Empirical distribution assuming iid observations. Includes the Kolmogorov-Smirnov statistic and tools to assess convergence of a random generator to a given statistical distribution.

Given some variable X, let F denote its cumulative distribution function on the real line [1]. Equivalently, F(X <= x) is the probability that X is less than or equal to x. Let X1,...,Xn denote a numbered random sample of X. The empirical distribution function is defined as

Fn(t) = (1/n) sum{1{Xi <= t} : i=1,...,n}


[1] Since F fully characterizes the distribution of X, is may be called the distribution of X i.e. the term cumulative may be omitted
